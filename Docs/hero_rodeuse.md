## Prompt de regeneration complete

```
Dark fantasy ink engraving illustration of a female Native American
ranger in her mid 20s. She is lean, athletic, wiry build, medium
height. Long STRAIGHT black hair flowing freely in the wind,
reaching past her shoulders. Proud, fierce expression - chin raised,
looking down at the viewer with quiet superiority. She knows she is
the deadliest person in the forest and does not need to prove it.

POSE: crouched on one knee on top of a large fallen tree trunk, one
foot planted on the trunk, the other leg hanging down. She holds a
wooden recurve bow VERTICALLY in her left hand, resting the bottom
tip on the trunk. Her right hand rests on her raised knee, relaxed
but alert. A quiver of mismatched arrows on her back. She is
surveying her territory from above, like a predator watching prey.

She wears worn dark leather armor with patches of green leaf and
bark camouflage. A dark green hooded cloak hangs from her shoulders,
pushed back. Leather hand wraps covering all fingers (no bare
fingers visible). Soft leather moccasin boots.

Style of Gustave Dore woodcut engraving with heavy crosshatching,
dramatic chiaroscuro. SERIOUS, DARK, REALISTIC tone - NOT cartoon.
Black ink on a flat smooth light COOL grey-beige background. No
paper grain, no fiber texture.

HATCHING STYLE IS CRITICAL: the hatching must be ORGANIC, IRREGULAR,
and HAND-MADE looking. Varying thickness, varying spacing, slightly
wobbly lines like a real hand-carved woodcut. NOT regular, NOT
parallel, NOT geometric. Contour lines must BLEND INTO the hatching.
NO "outline + fill" look. Overall ink coverage around 50%.

High contrast, limited palette: black ink with selective deep
emerald GREEN accent on: the crystal arrowhead of the nocked arrow
(glowing faintly green), the cloak interior lining, and a few
green-tinted leaves woven into her hair as decoration. These green
elements must be CLEARLY VISIBLE. NO color on the background forest.

BACKGROUND: vast ancient primeval forest, cathedral-like with
enormous moss-covered oak trunks rising like pillars into thick
canopy far above. Shafts of pale light filtering through gaps in
leaves. Dense ferns and undergrowth below. Thick fog rolling at
ground level. Narrow dirt path winding into misty depths. Render
with SPARSE bold IRREGULAR strokes - suggest the forest loosely.
Lots of empty light grey-beige space between the trees.

Sharp detailed face rendered PRIMARILY in black ink hatching like the rest of the body.
A very faint warm bronze/olive skin tone is visible as a WASH
underneath the hatching - like an old engraving lightly hand-tinted.
The BLACK INK HATCHING must DOMINATE the face, the skin color is
barely perceptible underneath. The face must NOT look more realistic
or painted than the rest of the illustration. High cheekbones, strong angular jaw, aquiline nose.
Sun-weathered skin, small scar on upper lip. Alert wide-set dark
eyes, intense and watchful. EXPRESSION: proud, calm, absolutely
still - a predator in perfect patience. No smile, no anger, just
cold certain focus. The face is the SHARPEST most detailed area.

Foreground: the thick mossy fallen tree trunk she crouches on, with
visible bark texture and shelf mushrooms. A dead rabbit tied to a
leather cord hanging from a branch. An old rusted hunting trap
half-buried in ferns.

Printmaking aesthetic, hand-carved feel. Landscape orientation,
3:2 aspect ratio.
```
