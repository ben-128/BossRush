# Azhda

## Identité

- **Nom :** Azhda
- **Nom complet (référence) :** Azhdaha
- **Difficulté :** Difficile
- **Culture d'origine :** Circassien (Caucase)
- **Référence mythologique :** Azhdaha — dragon du chaos et de la destruction des sagas Narts, ennemi juré des héros dans la mythologie circassienne

## Description visuelle

Dragon colossal à plusieurs têtes (3 ou 4). Corps serpentin massif couvert d'un mélange d'écailles sombres et de fourrure noire ébouriffée. Ailes immenses de chauve-souris déchirées. Chaque tête est différente : une crache du feu, une rugit, une mord, une murmure. Pattes griffues, queue épineuse. Yeux jaunes brûlants. Corruption : les écorchures et plaies entre les écailles laissent voir des veines de cristal noir pulsant.

## Stats

- **PV :** 11 + 4 par joueur (19/23/27/31)
- **Dégâts :** 3
- **Invocations :** 1
- **Passif :** *Plusieurs têtes* — Le boss attaque deux fois par tour (chaque tête frappe).
- **Action spéciale :** Une tête crache du feu : tous les héros subissent 1 dégât, ignore les boucliers/réductions.

## Sous-titre verso (lore)

- **FR :** *« Une tête pour chaque peuple qu'il gardait. »*
- **EN :** *« One head for each people he guarded. »*

## Prompt VERSO (DALL-E, impasto, 2:3 portrait)

```
Portrait vertical 2:3 format, wide shot showing full creature and environment. A colossal multi-headed dragon in the Caucasus mountains. Massive serpentine body covered in a mix of dark scales and shaggy black fur. Three or four heads, each different — one breathing fire, one roaring, one biting, one whispering. Huge tattered bat wings. Clawed paws, spined tail. Burning yellow eyes on each head. Black crystal corruption pulsing in cracks between the scales.

Setting: a dramatic Caucasus mountain pass, snow-capped peaks, ancient stone watchtowers in ruins, scattered armor and broken spears on the ground from defeated warriors, dark grey clouds, golden sunset light.

IMPORTANT STYLE: This painting is made of VISIBLE INDIVIDUAL THICK PAINT STROKES, like a Leonid Afremov painting or Van Gogh's Starry Night. Each stroke is a separate long thick ribbon of oil paint, clearly distinct from the next stroke. The paint looks like toothpaste squeezed in long lines onto the canvas. You should be able to count individual strokes one by one. Strokes have different sizes, directions, and thicknesses — never uniform. Some strokes are big and thick, others smaller. Strokes overlap chaotically, some areas have empty spaces between them where canvas shows. The paint builds form through deliberate directional lines, not blended smoothly. The entire image (character/creature AND background) must have the same thick paint stroke technique — not smoother on the figure. Palette: charcoal black, deep crimson, burnt orange, dark grey, golden sunset, cream highlights. Palette knife and thick brush technique. Dramatic lighting with strong light source creating highlights and shadows, warm rim light on the figure.

Forbidden: uniform texture, grain filter effect, smooth painting, evenly distributed brushwork, digital painting with impasto filter, AI-style neat strokes, smooth character on textured background.

The creature takes up about 60-70% of the frame, full body and all heads visible, environment with significant depth around it.
```
