# Prompt — Bottom texture (zone texte carte)

Inspiré des cartes Res Arcana : bord déchiré + ombre de transition + bords vieillis.

```
Torn parchment panel for a fantasy board game card, bottom section only. 
1024x1536 pixels, RGBA with transparent top ~35%.

The top edge is TORN/RIPPED — irregular, organic, hand-torn paper edge 
with slight curling and shadowing just below the tear. The tear line sits 
at roughly 35% from the top. Everything above the tear is fully transparent.

Below the tear:

BASE: warm cream/ivory painted surface with visible oil paint brushstrokes, 
impasto texture. Not flat — the paint has directional strokes, subtle ridges 
and thickness variation like real oil on canvas.

CRITICAL — inspired by Res Arcana card text panels:
- JUST BELOW THE TEAR: a darker band/shadow zone (burnt sienna, sepia tones) 
  that gradually fades into the lighter center. This shadow is 15-20% of the 
  visible area. It creates depth — like the torn edge casts a natural shadow, 
  or like paint was applied thicker near the transition. This is the key 
  difference with the current version.
- EDGES (left, right, bottom): slightly darker, warmer — burnt/aged look. 
  Uneven darkening, some spots more worn than others. Like an old document 
  handled many times.
- CENTER: the lightest area, where text will be placed. Warm ivory/cream. 
  Still has brushstroke texture but is the clearest zone for readability.
- BOTTOM EDGE: subtle darkening again, mirroring the top shadow but softer.
- SURFACE DETAILS: very faint foxing marks (tiny age spots), subtle 
  variations in cream tone (warmer patches, cooler patches), visible 
  canvas weave texture under the paint. Maybe 2-3 very faint ink stains.
- BRUSHSTROKE DIRECTION: mostly horizontal with some diagonal variation. 
  The strokes should feel hand-painted, not digitally filtered.

NO text, NO symbols, NO objects, NO borders, NO frames. 
Pure painted parchment texture only.

Style: oil painting on aged canvas, warm palette (cream, ivory, light ochre, 
touches of raw sienna). The feeling of a 200-year-old oil painting's surface.
```
